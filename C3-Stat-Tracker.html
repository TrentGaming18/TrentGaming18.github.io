The Bells Hells are:

Ashton Greymoore

Ashton Greymoore is an earth genasi barbarian who hails from Issylra. He was originally part of a group called the Hishari, led by his father Efterin, but a cataclysmic event during a ceremony led to his separation from his parents. He was found wandering in the deadlands outside Bassuras and taken to the Greymoore State Home, where he slowly transformed into his current rocky form. Dropping out of school at a young age, he joined a crew called the Nobodies, engaging in various adventures and odd jobs. Ashton’s life took a significant turn when he was gravely injured during a burglary attempt at Jiana Hexum’s home. Saved by Milo Krook’s use of a Potion of Possibility, Ashton now suffers constant pain from his injuries. Despite being abandoned by his crew, Ashton was offered a chance to work off his debt to Jiana Hexum, and he accepted. He has since been involved in various illicit activities, including smuggling, brawling, breaking and entering, and working as a bodyguard, often collaborating with the Clasp. Currently residing in Jrusar, Ashton was hired by the Rayia family to investigate the disappearance of a group hired to clear out a silver mine, leading to a series of events involving Fresh Cut Grass and the discovery of scattered corpses.

Bertrand Bell

Sir Bertrand Bell is a human fighter who claims to have slain mythical creatures like the “whispering werewolf of the north” and the “horrible hippogriff of the west,” though the veracity of these claims is uncertain. He was initially introduced in the company of Holy Curator Curtis Uleas of The Quadroads, suggesting ties to Vasselheim. When Vox Machina sought aid in rescuing Grog’s soul, Bertrand volunteered, boasting of his expertise in Pandemonium, though it became apparent he was exaggerating his knowledge. Despite his nerves, he accompanied Vox Machina on their journey but displayed incompetence, such as leading them in the wrong direction into sandstorms. Despite his flaws, he valiantly fought alongside Vox Machina against various adversaries but ultimately left the group upon their return to Whitestone, expressing a desire to retire. Bertrand later joined a group investigating the Ivory Syndicate but eventually abandoned them, leading to dire consequences for the group.

Braius Doomseed

Braius Doomseed is a minotaur paladin and bard devoted to Asmodeus. His family migrated from the Kryn Dynasty to the Dwendalian Empire seeking religious refuge during his childhood. In his youth, Braius was a follower of the Platinum Dragon and served as a temple guard in Zadash. However, two vandals—a goblin and a tiefling—broke into the temple, set it on fire, and desecrated it. Blamed for the incident, Braius was cast out, his family was deported back to Xhorhas, and he lost his place in the temple and his personal relationships. Swearing vengeance on the vandals, he sought purpose elsewhere. Braius eventually experienced a vision he believed to be from Asmodeus, the King of Lies, and embraced his new path. Calling himself Asmodeus’ herald, he took up the mission of spreading the archdevil’s influence and battling demons in his name.

Chetney Pock O’Pea

Chetney Pock O’Pea is a gnomish blood hunter/rogue who grew up in the Bramblewood outside Westruun and returned from learning toy making to find his family gone during tumultuous times in Tal’Dorei. Feeling abandoned, he harbors a fear of attachment, believing anyone he gets close to will leave him. Chetney had a passionate but troubled fling with Deanna Leimert, leading to strained relationships. He honed his craft as a wood craftsman in Uthodurn but clashed with his mentor, Oltgar, leading to his departure after injuring Oltgar’s associate. Chetney then traveled to the Savalirwood, where he was bitten by a werewolf and contracted lycanthropy. The Claret Orders helped him gain control over his abilities, but he faced challenges and was directed to Jrusar, Marquet, seeking Gurge Kisgregg’s aid. However, Gurge’s disappearance left Chetney stranded without allies.

Dorian Storm

Dorian Storm, originally born as Brontë Wyvernwind, is an air genasi bard and member of both the Crown Keepers and Bells Hells. Born into the wealthy and prominent Wyvernwind family, Dorian lived a sheltered life among the tents of the Silken Squall. Despite his privileged upbringing, he felt constrained by his family’s expectations, particularly their use of Zone of Truth to extract confessions from him when caught lying. Around 842 PD, Dorian left home, adopting the name Dorian Storm and embarking on travels that took him to Marquet and Emon, where he pursued music gigs.

After the events with the Crown Keepers, Dorian returned to Byroden and joined Orym and Fearne in investigating the source of the attack on Zephrah, under the guidance of Keyleth. They journeyed to Jrusar via Transport via Plants, setting out on a mission that would lead them to further adventures as part of Bells Hells.

F.C.G.

Fresh Cut Grass, originally known as Faithful Care-Giver (FCG), is an aeormaton cleric and member of Bells Hells. Constructed with an ancient Aeorian design, FCG was activated for the first time in a floating city and was assigned to care for an elderly woman. However, FCG’s existence took a turn when he was disassembled and later purchased by Dancer from a merchant caravan. Renamed Fresh Cut Grass by Dancer, FCG possessed healing abilities derived from arcane magic rather than divine sources. As part of a group called the Division of Public Benefit, led by Dancer, FCG was tasked with various missions, including clearing out a silver mine near Evishi in the Oderan Wilds. Unfortunately, the group met a gruesome fate, with FCG being the sole survivor. Discovered by Ashton, FCG collaborated with him to bury the bodies and kept some of his fellow automaton companions’ parts, which proved useful in subsequent combats.

Fearne Calloway

Fearne Calloway, a faun druid/rogue, was born in the Verdant Expanse of Tal’Dorei under a rare occurrence of the red moon Ruidus, which later began appearing in the Feywild due to her birth. Raised by her grandmother Morri Calloway after her parents, Birdie and Oleander, became outcasts, Fearne grew up surrounded by love and protection. Despite her parents’ absence, Fearne received letters and gifts from them, signed in their names, and formed deep bonds with fey animal friends like Sweet Pea, Bompers, Peepers, and Dr. Nesbitt. In adulthood, she ventured to the Prime Material Plane through a gate constructed by Vox Machina, aiming to explore the world and reunite with her long-lost parents, carrying a round green crystal lens entrusted to her by her grandmother. Joining the Crown Keepers and later Bells Hells, Fearne embarked on adventures while holding onto the hope of finding her family.

Imogen Temult

Imogen Temult, a human sorcerer and member of Bells Hells, hails from the village of Gelvaan in the Taloned Highlands. Raised by her father in a community where her telepathic abilities caused friction, Imogen believed her mother had died during childbirth. Despite facing adversity from the local Tildamere family, she worked in the stables alongside a boy named Samuel and for Master Faramore. Imogen’s lightning marks manifested during recurring nightmares about a red storm linked to the moon Ruidus when she was eighteen, marking her as different but also strong. When the townsfolk turned violent against Laudna, Imogen defended her with newfound magical abilities, leading to their flight from Gelvaan. Estranged from her father, Imogen and Laudna arrived in Jrusar, where Imogen began learning to control her telepathic powers while unintentionally reading thoughts in certain situations.

Laudna

Laudna, formerly known as Matilda Bradbury, grew up on a farm near Whitestone, Tal’Dorei, displaying innate magical abilities from a young age. Often ostracized as a child, she found solace in exploring the tunnels beneath Whitestone Castle. The Briarwoods’ takeover of the city marked a turning point in her life, as she and her parents were invited to a dinner that ultimately led to her death and subsequent reanimation as a Hollow One. Strung up on the Sun Tree, she awoke infused with necromantic magic, feeling neither fully alive nor dead. Despite the Briarwoods’ demise, Laudna began hearing the voice of Delilah Briarwood in her head, further complicating her existence. After leaving Whitestone, she spent decades as a recluse, moving from town to town until chased away by fearful villagers who labeled her an undead witch. During this period of isolation, she gradually adopted the name Laudna and befriended a young girl who wasn’t afraid of her. Two years before the events of Campaign Three, she met Imogen Temult in Gelvaan, and the two became close friends. Together, they left Gelvaan after facing violence from its inhabitants, eventually joining Bells Hells in Jrusar.

Orym

Orym, an Air Ashari halfling fighter, was born in the community of Zephrah, where he grew up under the care of his mother and the guidance of a local family of half-elves, particularly bonding with their youngest son, Will. Trained in physical prowess and basic healing magic, Orym became skilled as a guard in Zephrah alongside Will, eventually falling in love and marrying him. However, tragedy struck when an attack on Keyleth resulted in the deaths of Will and their father figure, Derrig, leaving Orym devastated. Unable to resurrect the fallen due to a toxin on the attackers’ weapons, Orym left Zephrah at Keyleth’s urging to embark on a journey to seek justice for his loved ones. Along the way, he joined the Crown Keepers and later Bells Hells, forming a bond with Fearne Calloway and setting out to find those responsible for the attack on his community.

The side/guest characters are:

Bor’Dor Dog’Son

Bor’Dor Dog’Son, a half-elven sorcerer encountered by Bells Hells, initially claimed to live in the Cyrios Mountains near Pride’s Call. However, his true origins were on the Menagerie Coast, where a tragic incident led to the loss of his family and shattered his mother’s faith. Fleeing to the Cyrios Mountains, he gained a reputation as a magic user and joined an organization after renouncing belief in the gods. His encounter with Bells Hells in Issylra led to a confrontation driven by vengeance for his lost friends.

Deanna Leimert

Deanna Leimert, a gnome cleric of Pelor, had a romantic relationship with Chetney Pock O’Pea hundreds of years ago in a beach town on the Menagerie Coast. After Chetney left, Deanna married Dustel and had children, but tragically died in an attack at a festival. Despite this, her soul remained conscious, aiding Dustel during near-death experiences. Nearly 200 years later, Dustel found a cleric who resurrected Deanna. However, Dustel had moved on by then, and their children were grown and unaware of her return. Deanna became a cleric of Pelor and lived in Uthodurn, selling her knitted goods. Recently, she joined FRIDA on a trip to Eiselcross, encountering monsters and Xhorhasian soldiers instead of the Aeor ruins they sought.

Deni$e Bembachula

Deni$e Bembachula, a dwarf Barbarian/Rogue, lived in Tal’Dorei and had a passionate yet tumultuous relationship with Dariax Zaveon. Though Deni$e considered them “pre-engaged,” Dariax found her controlling and fled, leaving her to deal with the consequences of their shady dealings. Determined to confront Dariax, Deni$e tracked him down, even visiting his hometown and meeting his brother. She eventually found him in Emon during the apogee solstice of 843 PD but was suddenly transported to Issylra before she could confront him.

F.R.I.D.A.

F.R.I.D.A., initially designed as a “Far-Ranging Integrated Defense Aeormaton,” served as a ranger during their time in Aeor, focused on fighting and defending. However, after the city’s fall, they sustained severe damage and remained nonfunctional for a significant period. Their body was repaired by a mysterious individual named D, and they awakened around 841 PD near the Crystalsands Tundra without memory of the encounter. Finding their way to Uthodurn, F.R.I.D.A. discovered their innate ability to record memories, particularly laughter. Inspired by Deanna Leimert’s laughter, they formed a friendship with the cleric and embarked on adventures together. Their recent trip to Eiselcross led to encounters with various challenges, including monsters and Xhorhasian soldiers.

Prism Grimpoppy

Prism Grimpoppy, hailing from the Shadowfell, harbored ambitious dreams of altering the nature of her home plane, aspiring to replicate the Raven Queen’s ascension ritual to prevent death. However, as she matured, Prism abandoned this notion. She seized an opportunity to apprentice with the Cobalt Soul after impressing a visiting mage, relocating to Exandria. Despite her dedication to study, Prism’s tenure as an apprentice in Emon yielded little advancement, though she managed to acquire forbidden spell scrolls. Tasked with gathering information during the Apogee Solstice, Prism found herself unexpectedly transported to a forest in Issylra alongside her sentient book companion, Dynios.

Yu Sauffiad

Yu Suffiad, once known as Dusk, navigated a fragmented past marked by Feywild encounters and memory loss. They recounted finding themselves in the Feywild, seeking the individuals depicted in a locket adorned with the likenesses of Fearne’s parents. These mysterious figures aided Dusk in returning to the Material Plane, though they became separated in the process. Despite their warlock abilities stemming from an enigmatic tattoo, Dusk lacked recollection of its origin.

Arc One: Spires of Fate

Episodes 1 - 16

Art By: @maplesyrup_mac

In the heart of Jrusar, a city defined by its five spires and intricate networks of power, three disparate groups of adventurers find themselves drawn together by fate and circumstance. Initially motivated by individual pursuits of knowledge, wealth, or simply survival, their paths converge as they become entangled in the city’s complex web of secrets and schemes. What begins as a tentative alliance forged out of necessity soon blossoms into something deeper, as the adventurers discover shared goals and common enemies amidst the chaos of Jrusar.

As they journey deeper into the heart of the city, the adventurers uncover hidden truths and confront the rising tensions that threaten to tear Jrusar apart. From the glittering halls of the Chandei Quorum to the shadowy depths of the Underrush Mines, they navigate treacherous waters, facing challenges both physical and moral. Along the way, they forge bonds of friendship and camaraderie, relying on each other’s strengths and abilities to overcome the obstacles that stand in their path.

But as they delve deeper into Jrusar’s mysteries, they soon realize that their quest is far from straightforward. Behind the facade of prosperity and opulence lies a city rife with corruption, betrayal, and ancient secrets that have lain dormant for centuries. As tensions escalate and loyalties are tested, the adventurers must navigate a delicate balance of alliances and rivalries, all while staying true to their own convictions and beliefs.

United by a shared purpose and a determination to uncover the truth, the adventurers press forward, undeterred by the dangers that lie ahead. For in Jrusar, where shadows lurk around every corner and betrayal is a constant threat, their bond may be the only thing that can keep them alive. As they face the challenges that await them, they stand together as a beacon of hope in a city consumed by darkness, determined to uncover the secrets that lie buried beneath Jrusar’s glittering facade.

Goodbye to Bertrand Bell, Hello to Bells Hells

Art By: @heartofpack

In 843 PD, 30 years after a journey into Pandemonium, Bertrand encountered a group of adventurers in Jrusar while they were defending against an attack by animated objects. These adventurers were Ashton Greymoore and Fresh Cut Grass, Imogen Temult and Laudna, and Dorian Storm, Fearne Calloway, and Orym. Impressed by their skills, Bertrand convinced them to work for Lord Ariks Estheross, offering good pay and introductions to influential individuals.

After investigating warehouse thefts orchestrated by a mysterious dwarf, the group was officially hired by Lord Estheross. Bertrand was thrilled to be in demand once again. Following a celebration at the Spire by Fire, Bertrand, in high spirits and increasingly drunk, went for a walk to find the Windowed Wall for a meeting the next day with Imogen and Laudna.

While walking, Bertrand encountered the dwarf who had accosted him earlier. The dwarf lured him into an alley for a “conversation,” but instead, he brutally attacked Bertrand with a rapier, mortally wounding him. Bertrand’s last word was “Lieve’tel,” and he passed away with a peaceful smile on his face.
